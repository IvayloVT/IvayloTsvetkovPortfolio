User Experience Report: Pizza Uno Website

This study report provides a comprehensive breakdown of the Pizza Uno Sunderland website, analyzing its structure, content, and user experience across its five primary pages.

1. Home Page (index.html)

The Home page serves as the digital storefront, focusing on brand identity and immediate value propositions.

  • Visual Branding: Features a prominent wood-fired pizza theme.
  • Key Messaging: Emphasizes authenticity and fresh ingredients.
  • Promotional Offers:
    • Family Feast: £29.99
    • Meal Deal: £19.99
    • Any Large Pizza: £9.99 (Collection)
  • Functionality: Live "Your Basket" sidebar and GDPR cookie consent.
Figure 1: Home Page (index.html)

2. Order Online (order.html)

The functional core designed for menu browsing and selection.

  • User Interface: JavaScript-powered "Add to Basket" buttons with real-time price calculation.
  • Basket Integration: Displays subtotal and a flat £2.50 delivery fee.
Figure 2: Order Online (order.html)

3. Feedback (feedback.html)

Engagement page designed to gather customer satisfaction data.

  • Form Fields: Name, Email, and a text area for reviews.
  • Purpose: Building brand "social proof."
Figure 3: Feedback (feedback.html)

4. Contact Us (contact.html)

Logistical information and communication channels.

  • Location: Specific targeting for the Sunderland area.
  • Tools: Map integration (Google Maps) for collection assistance.
Figure 4: Contact Us (contact.html)

5. Login / Account (login.html)

Gateway for returning customers to manage history.

  • Retention: Save delivery addresses and "favorite" orders for faster checkout.
Figure 5: Login / Account (login.html)

6. Summary of Technical & Business Observations

Technology Stack HTML5, CSS3, and Vanilla JavaScript.
Hosting GitHub Pages (Static architecture).
Business Model High-volume meal deals and collection incentives.
User Experience Mobile-first responsive design.

7. Conclusion

The Pizza Uno website is a highly efficient, "no-nonsense" e-commerce tool. It prioritizes speed and clarity over flashy animations. For a local restaurant, this is an ideal setup: it tells the user what the food looks like, how much it costs, and how to get it in under three clicks.
